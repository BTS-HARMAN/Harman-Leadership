Executive Summary
Key Findings
1
The top rated behaviors are internal and execution-focused; the bottom are external and future-facing
The five highest-rated behaviors are all Integrity and Ownership: internal and execution-focused. The five lowest are all Innovation and externally oriented. The framework is intentionally weighted toward the external, future-facing behaviors HARMAN needs to build.
2
Highest-rated behaviors were table stakes and have been removed from the framework
The highest-rated behaviors (adhering to laws and standards, owning mistakes, treating others with equal respect) were rated critical by 93 to 94% of respondents across every cut of the data. Because these reflect universally held employment expectations rather than differentiating leadership behaviors, they have been removed from the framework.
3
Innovation ranked lowest across every cut of the data and all behaviors were kept in the framework
Innovation behaviors ranked lowest in every cut of the data. Brings outside ideas to HARMAN scored 75% (WC) and 76% (BC), the lowest single behavior in the dataset. The framework retains all innovation behaviors. Low scores signal a capability HARMAN is actively building: that is precisely why they belong here.
4
The data validates one unified global framework
The same behaviors surfaced at the top and bottom across every region, gender, division, and span of control. Variation was in magnitude, not direction. This holds even across divisions: Automotive, Corporate, and Lifestyle all converged on the same top and bottom behaviors. The data supports that a single unified framework can land with credibility across the organization.
5
Leaders with more direct reports rate behaviors higher, but prioritize the same ones
Leaders with 10 or more direct reports rated behaviors 7 to 14 points more favorably than individual contributors, but prioritized the same ones. The largest gaps (questioning the status quo, putting team goals first, sharing information proactively) point to where development should start earlier, not where the framework should differ.
